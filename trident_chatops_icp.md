# Trident ChatOps — Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) & Market Positioning

## Target Customer Analysis

### AT&T / Large Service Providers — Yes, but at the edge

AT&T and similar carriers won't replace their core NMS (Netcool, ServiceNow, custom OSS/BSS
with billions invested). But they have a real problem at the field engineer / NOC L1 level:

- Field techs troubleshooting CPE routers need quick answers without VPN-ing into 5 different tools
- L1 NOC agents handling customer fault calls need guided workflows
- Trident ChatOps fits as a lightweight front-end for field ops — not replacing backbone OSS/BSS

**Positioning:** Complementary field operations tool, not a core NMS replacement.

---

### Cisco TAC — Wrong Target

Cisco TAC engineers are senior and already have internal tooling. Cisco itself builds platforms
like NSO and CX Cloud. Cisco as a reseller/partner channel is more interesting than Cisco as
a direct customer.

---

### SMBs / MSPs — The Sweet Spot (Primary ICP)

The strongest product-market fit is with smaller organisations that:
- Cannot afford enterprise NMS platforms ($50K-$200K+/yr)
- Have small NOC teams with limited senior engineer availability
- Need junior staff to handle L1/L2 reliably without breaking things
- Win or lose contracts based on SLA and operational cost

---

## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

| Segment | Profile | Why They Buy |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| Network MSP | 10-200 devices/customer, 5-50 customers | L1 enablement, margin protection |
| Regional ISP / Tier-2 Carrier | 500-5000 CPE devices, small NOC | MTTR reduction, SLA protection, NOC cost |
| Mid-market Enterprise IT | 20-200 devices, 500-5000 employees | No NMS budget, need senior output from junior staff |
| System Integrators (Managed Services) | Presidio, Logicalis, Dimension Data type | Differentiator in bids, cost efficiency at scale |

---

## Segment Details

### 1. Network VAR / MSP (Highest Priority)
- Managing 50-500 customer sites across Cisco, Arista, Juniper gear
- Support 20-30 SMB clients with a small team
- Cannot justify a senior engineer on every account
- Trident ChatOps lets 2 L1 engineers do the work of 6
- Key metric: tickets resolved per engineer per day

### 2. Regional ISP / Tier-2 Carrier
- 500-2000 CPE devices under management
- Small NOC teams, thin margins, SLA-driven contracts
- MTTR reduction directly protects revenue without headcount increase
- Key metric: SLA breach rate, MTTR, NOC cost per device

### 3. Mid-Market Enterprise IT
- 20-100 network devices (campus, DC, WAN)
- One senior network engineer, several junior IT staff
- Senior engineer bottlenecked by L1/L2 tasks
- Need junior staff to safely handle faults with guardrails
- Key metric: escalations to senior engineer per week

### 4. System Integrators (Managed Services Division)
- Companies like Presidio, Logicalis, Dimension Data
- Manage infrastructure for large enterprises under SLA
- Win contracts on cost and SLA performance
- Trident ChatOps improves both simultaneously
- Key metric: gross margin per managed customer

## Winning Sales Message

**Do NOT say:** "Replace your NMS"  — hard sell, triggers procurement battles.

**DO say:**
> "Your L1 engineer resolves in 15 minutes what used to take a senior engineer 90 minutes.
> Without buying Cisco NSO."

This resonates with VP of Operations, NOC Manager, and IT Director personas who feel
the daily pain of L1/L2 bottlenecks and escalation overhead.

---

## Secondary / Future Targets

- **Field Service Organisations** — Technicians dispatched to customer sites needing
  remote device visibility without full NMS access
- **University / Research Networks** — Small teams managing large campus networks
- **Government / Defense IT** — Air-gapped deployments, strict audit requirements
  (Trident ChatOps runs fully on-premise with no external dependencies)
